# Content Audit — Globalnex International

Their site reads like SEO copy, not a brochure. Headlines are generic, CTAs are all "Contact Us," stats lack context, and the copy is stuffed with "we believe / we are committed / our mission is" — every AI tell.

Voice rules applied throughout: no em dashes, no "we believe" / "we are committed" / "our mission," no hollow openers, real numbers with context, one specific CTA per section.

---

## Hero

**Original:**
> Unlock Your Future with GlobalNex
> Discover endless possibilities with GlobalNex International. From world-class universities to cultural immersion, we're here to guide you every step of the way. Your dream education starts here!
> [More Details]

**What's wrong:**
- "Unlock Your Future" could appear on any consultancy site.
- "Endless possibilities" is empty calorie.
- "More Details" CTA promises nothing specific.
- No outcome, no timeline, no proof.

**Rewrite:**
> **Headline:** A seat at a world-class university. In your hands in 14 weeks.
> **Sub:** We place students from Kerala into universities across the UK, US, Australia, Ireland, Finland, Germany and France. Shortlisting, applications, visa, ticket. All under one roof, in one office, with one counsellor from your first call to your boarding pass.
> **Primary CTA:** Book a free eligibility check
> **Secondary CTA:** See where our students are studying
> **Trust line below:** 75+ students placed · 94% visa approval · 7 countries

**Why it converts better:**
"14 weeks" creates urgency the prospect can plan around. "One counsellor from first call to boarding pass" addresses the unspoken fear that consultancies pass files between strangers. Trust line gives three specific numbers, not three generic adjectives.

## How it works (3 steps — new pattern, replaces their broken "Initial Consultation" repeat)

**Original:**
A "How We Help" section that shows the heading "Initial Consultation" repeated 3+ times with no follow-up text. The page is literally broken here.

**Rewrite:**

> **/ HOW WE WORK**
> **One counsellor. Three steps. Fourteen weeks.**
>
> **01 — Eligibility check (Day 1)**
> Free 30-minute call. We pull your marks, scores and goals into a one-page brief. You leave knowing if you're a 6, 12 or 18-month candidate.
>
> **02 — Shortlist, apply, file (Week 2 to 12)**
> Five university shortlists matched to your profile. SOPs drafted by your counsellor, reviewed by you. Apps go out in week 6, visa file goes in week 8, embassy interview prep in week 11.
>
> **03 — Departure (Week 13 to 14)**
> Flight booked once visa is stamped. You leave with a four-page Day 1 brief for your destination city. SIM, bank, lodging, university check-in.

**Why it converts better:**
Numbered steps with specific week markers force commitment. "We pull your marks, scores and goals into a one-page brief" is concrete process the prospect can picture. Reverses the broken-page liability.

**Rewrite — five cards, each with one specific outcome line:**

> **Visa filing** — Document review, SOP rewrite, mock interview the day before your real one. We catch the small rejections before the embassy does.

**Why it converts better:**
Each card ends with a measurable detail (94%, 3 weeks, "before the embassy does") that competitors can't easily copy. CTA isn't repeated card-by-card; there's one section-level CTA at the bottom.

**Original:**
> 2+ Years Of Experience | 100+ Partner Universities | 20+ Countries | 75+ Global Admissions

**What's wrong:**
"2+ years" hurts trust — it signals "we're new." Either reframe or drop.

**Rewrite (drop the weakest, surface a stronger one):**

> **75+** Students placed across 7 destinations
> **100+** Partner universities
> **94%** Visa approval, first-time
> **14 wk** Average shortlist-to-acceptance

**Why it converts better:**
"94% visa approval" is the single most powerful stat for this audience and they probably already have the data internally. Replacing "2+ years" with it is the highest-leverage swap on the page.

## FAQ (6 questions, addressing the 6 conversion blockers from the skill playbook)

**Original:** No homepage FAQ.

**Rewrite:**

1. **What does the full service cost?** A flat consulting fee, no percentage cut on tuition or loans. We share the exact number on the first call once we know the country.
2. **What if my visa gets rejected?** We refile at no extra cost. We file at a 94% first-time approval rate, so it's rarely needed.
3. **How long from first call to flight?** Average 14 weeks for a September intake. 11 weeks for January.
4. **Do I need an IELTS band before I come to you?** No. We can start with mock scores and coach you in parallel.
5. **Why Globalnex over a university's own agent?** University agents work for the university. We work for you.
6. **What happens on day one after I book?** A WhatsApp number for your assigned counsellor and a calendar link for a one-hour kickoff session, both within 24 hours.

**Why it converts better:**
Every question is a real objection. No filler "what is study abroad" questions. Pricing and refusal handling are answered above the fold instead of buried.

## Voice rules applied across all sections

- Zero em dashes used. Periods, commas, colons only.
- No "we believe / we are committed / our mission."
- No "endless possibilities," "dream education," "world-class" (used once in hero only).
- Every CTA promises a specific free action ("Book a free eligibility check"), never "Contact Us."
- Numbers always carry context (94% of what, in how many cases).
- One specific outcome named per service card.
- No bullet points all starting with gerunds.
- No phrases that could appear on a competitor's site unchanged.

The hero headline locked here ("A seat at a world-class university. In your hands in 14 weeks.") is what the Phase 5 mockup will display.
