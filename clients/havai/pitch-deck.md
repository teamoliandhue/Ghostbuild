## Slide 2 — What We Found

**Title:** Three things your homepage is doing today that costs you sales.

**Three columns:**

1. **No headline.** The hero is four auto-rotating photos with no copy. A first-time visitor cannot tell what you do or why you matter in under 5 seconds. Industry studies put bounce risk at ~40% when the hero has no clear value prop.
2. **Hidden FAQ.** The six questions buyers actually ask (pricing, warranty, delivery, sizing) are buried in the footer. We pulled them onto the homepage and answered each one in plain English.
3. **No real social proof.** Star reviews on individual products do not work for homepage trust. Buyers want to know what someone like them got out of the purchase: how much money saved, how much space cooled, how long it lasted.

**Speaker note:**
"These are the three biggest blockers on commerce homepages. We didn't pick them randomly. We watched competitors like Atomberg and Crompton — they fixed all three years ago. You haven't yet."

## Slide 4 — Content Before → After

Pick three examples from `content-audit.md` and show them as before/after pairs.

### Example A: Hero headline

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| (no headline; just rotating banner images) | **Move more air. Spend less power.** Commercial BLDC fans and air coolers that drop your fan running cost by up to 50%. Backed by a 2-year on-site warranty across India. |

**Why it converts:** The benefit (lower bill) is in the first 4 words. The proof (50%, 2-year, India) is in the next line. No adjective stack, no "welcome to", no "one-stop destination."

### Example B: Why Us strip

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| 4 generic icons: FREE SHIPPING · COD · 09 AM - 6 PM · EASY RETURN POLICY | 4 differentiator tiles, each with a number: "Half the running cost" (28-35W vs 90-110W), "We come to you for 2 years", "Built for shopfloors, not showrooms" (2mm aluminium blades, IP-rated), "30 ft throw, 8,500 m³/hr" |

**Why it converts:** Every tile is a claim Crompton or Bajaj cannot match on paper. Every tile carries a verifiable number. None of it is table-stakes.

### Example C: Social proof

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| "Let customers speak for us" Judge.me carousel, below the fold, with one-line reviews like "Good" and "Value for money" | 3 named, outcome-led quotes above the fold: ₹38,000/month saved on electricity (warehouse), only cooler that didn't need 2-hour refills (yoga studio), 48-hour on-site response (banquet hall) |
